## Scenario
## You are testing whether a difference exists
## in the frequency of colds among 18–30 year olds
## who are meat-eaters and vegetarians.

## MY SOLUTION (ordered steps):

## Steps to Order
## - Define the population: adults aged 18 to 30 who eat meat or are vegetarian.
## - Define the null hypothesis: there is no difference in the frequency of colds among meat-eaters and vegetarians.
## - Define the alternative hypothesis: young adults who eat meat experience more colds.
## - Collect the data: diet status and number of colds in the last six months.
## - Perform statistical tests on the sample data: difference in mean number of colds between the two populations.
## - Draw conclusions about the population: are the frequency of colds different for young adults who consume meat?
